Teaching

University of Oxford

  • Prelims. Political Analysis (Q-Step 1).
    [Course Description]
    Introductory statistics and quantitative methods for first-year Philosophy, Politics and Economics (PPE) and History and Politics (HP) undergraduates. Topics include research design, causal inference, and applied data analysis in R. Teaching delivered in lecture format.
  • 205. Government and Politics of the United States.
    [Course Description]
    American political institutions, electoral behavior, Congress, the presidency, the federal government, interest groups, courts, and constitutional law for second and third-year PPE and HP undergraduates. Teaching delivered in seminar tutorial format.
  • 220. Political Sociology.
    [Course Description]
    Covering the social basis of political behavior for second-year PPE and HP undergraduates. Topics include social capital, social attitudes, social movements, nationalism, and civil wars. Teaching delivered in seminar tutorial format.
  • Revisions. Political Sociology, Jesus College & St. Peter's College.
    [Course Description]
    Revision tutorials for final-year PPE and PH students preparing for finalist examinations.
